03

MET is the tangible sequel to Madrid: it turns an agreed principle into shared measurement infrastructure. Scope is bounded to measurement and reporting to remain a research instrument, not regulated clinical software.

What it does
RESEARCH INSTRUMENT

Applies a faculty-ratified landmark protocol to compute absolute extrusion (mm) and the Extrusion Index (%), then outputs a standardized, comparable report — the precondition for every centralization outcome study.

What it will not do
REGULATORY BOUNDARY

No diagnosis, no candidacy flag, no treatment or product recommendation. Crossing into patient-specific advice would make it Software as a Medical Device — a separate regulated pathway.

Tokyo deliverable
SUMMIT OUTCOME

A ratified annotation standard from the knee faculty; this working prototype embodying it; committed sites to contribute labelled MRIs toward validation reported at Paris 2027.

Path to validation
POST-SUMMIT

Multi-centre annotated dataset → automated segmentation model → inter-reader reliability study → device-agnostic outcomes registry on the standardized endpoint.
